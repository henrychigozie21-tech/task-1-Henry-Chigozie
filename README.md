Problem Definition

This week focused on understanding the user deeply through structured research methods and translating those insights into clear problem statements that guide design decisions.

User Persona Development

I created a detailed user persona to represent a key target user:

Tolu Adekunle

* Location: Abuja, Nigeria
* Occupation: Business Development Executive
* Income: ₦350,000/month
* Behavior: Heavy phone user, active on social media, attends virtual meetings frequently

Key Goals:

* Improve mental clarity and reduce stress
* Maintain a sustainable daily habit
* Avoid overwhelming or time-consuming routines
* Prefer quick, simple solutions (e.g., short sessions like “5-minute workouts”)

Empathy Mapping

To better understand the user’s mindset, I mapped out their:

* Thoughts & Feelings:
    Feels overwhelmed, struggles with consistency, wants to be productive but lacks time
* Says & Does:
    Talks about wanting to improve routines but often postpones action
* Pain Points:
    * Feels guilty for not staying consistent
    * Experiences burnout from long or complex routines
    * Struggles with motivation after work hours
* Needs:
    Simplicity, flexibility, and low-effort solutions that fit into a busy schedule

Frustrations & Barriers

 Frustrations:

* Apps require 30–60 minutes commitment
* Gamified fitness experiences feel exhausting rather than motivating
* Inconsistency leads to self-doubt and frustration
* Gym memberships feel wasteful when unused

Barriers to Entry:

* Busy work schedule (8AM–5PM)
* Decision fatigue after work hours
* Long commute times
* Overly complex app interfaces


Daily Routine Insights

* Starts work at 8AM
* Has virtual meetings throughout the day
* Commutes for ~45 minutes
* Evenings are mentally draining
* Weekends are occupied with errands

Key Insight:
The user has very limited energy and time, especially after work meaning any solution must be quick, frictionless, and easy to start.

Problem Statement

Busy professionals like Tolu struggle to maintain consistent self-care or fitness routines because existing solutions are time-consuming, overwhelming, and difficult to integrate into their daily lives.


Key Insights

* Users prefer short, achievable activities over long sessions
* Consistency > intensity for habit formation
* Emotional factors (guilt, burnout) play a major role in user behavior
* Simplicity and accessibility are critical for engagement


Outcome

This research phase provided a strong, user-centered foundation for the design process. The insights gathered will guide the development of solutions that are:

* Time-efficient
* Easy to adopt
* Emotionally supportive
* Designed for real-life routines
